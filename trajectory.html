class jdrones.trajectory.FifthOrderPolynomialTrajectory(start_pos: tuple[float, float, float], dest_pos: tuple[float, float, float], T: float = 5, start_vel: tuple[float, float, float] = (0, 0, 0), dest_vel: tuple[float, float, float] = (0, 0, 0), start_acc: tuple[float, float, float] = (0, 0, 0), dest_acc: tuple[float, float, float] = (0, 0, 0))[source]

Bases: BasePolynomialTrajectory

A helper class to solve the quintic polynomial given a start and end criterion.

Define the problem as

\[\begin{split}\begin{align} x &= c_0 t ^5 + c_1 t^4 + c_2 t ^3 + c_3 t ^2 + c_4 t + c_5,\\ \dot x &= 5 c_0 t ^ 4 + 4 c_1 t^3 + 3 c_2 t ^2 + 2 c_3 t + c_4,\\ \ddot x &= 20 c_0 t ^ 3 + 12 c_1 t^2 + 6 c_2 t + 2 c_3 \end{align}\end{split}\]

Which is then solved by

\[\begin{split}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\ T^5 & T^4 & T^3 & T^2 & T & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 5T^4 & 4T^3 & 3T^2 & 2T & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 20T^3 & 12T^2 & 6T & 2 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_{t=0}\\ x_{t=T}\\ \dot x_{t=0}\\ \dot x_{t=T}\\ \ddot x_{t=0}\\ \ddot x_{t=T} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} c_0\\ c_1\\ c_2\\ c_3\\ c_4\\ c_5\\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Where \(T\) is the total time to traverse the polynomial.

See also

jdrones.envs.FifthOrderPolyPositionDroneEnv.calc_traj()

The coefficients are then saved in coeffs and accessed by acceleration(),:meth:velocity, and position() as required.

Note

The trajectory is solved individually for \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\).

class jdrones.trajectory.FirstOrderPolynomialTrajectory(start_pos: tuple[float, float, float], dest_pos: tuple[float, float, float], T: float = 5)[source]

Bases: BasePolynomialTrajectory

A helper class to solve the first-order polynomial given a start and end criterion.

Define the problem as

\[x = c_0 t + c_1\]

Which is then solved by

\[\begin{split}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ T & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_{t=0}\\ x_{t=T} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} c_0\\ c_1 \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Where \(T\) is the total time to traverse the polynomial.

See also

jdrones.envs.FirstOrderPolyPositionDroneEnv.calc_traj()

The coefficients are then saved in coeffs and accessed by acceleration(),:meth:velocity, and position() as required.

Note

The trajectory is solved individually for \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\).
